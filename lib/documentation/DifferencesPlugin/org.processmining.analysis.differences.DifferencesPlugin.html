The differences analysis plugin allows you to discover and diagnose differences between two business processes either in the Petri net or in the EPC notation.

References

[1] R.M. Dijkman. Feedback on Differences between Business Processes. BETA Working Paper WP-234, Eindhoven University of Technology, Eindhoven, The Netherlands, 2007.

[2] R.M. Dijkman. A Classification of Differences between Similar Business Processes. In: Proceedings of the 11th IEEE EDOC Conference (EDOC), Annapolis, MD, USA, pp. 37-47, 2007.

Overview of the Approach

The plugin takes two processes as input and decides whether they are different. (We say that two processes are different if they are not completed trace equivalent.) If the processes are different, the plugin points out exactly where they are different and why they are different. This in contrast to techniques that provide simple true/false answers about whether two processes are different or not. The feedback on why processes are different is given in terms of frequently occurring differences that we discovered in practice. The plugin can be used for various purposes, such as detecting differences between processes in a merger between organizations.

Using the Plugin

The following procedure shows you how to use the plug-in:

  1. open the processes that you want to compare, for this example use componentnet1.pnml and componentnet2.pnml;
  2. open this plugin, use the two processes that you just opened as arguments;
  3. now indicate which tasks in one process are equivalent to which tasks in the other process, for this example tasks with identical names are considered to be equivalent, if you click 'find match' the plugin will automatically find a match between tasks based on their labels;
  4. click 'evaluate' and the plugin will identify and diagnose the differences.
